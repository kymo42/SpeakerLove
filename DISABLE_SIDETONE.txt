WHAT IS SIDETONE?
═════════════════

Sidetone = Your own voice being sent back to your speakers
Happens when: Microphone is configured to "Monitor this device"

Result: You hear yourself speaking while your teammates also hear you
Problem: Confusing and annoying during gaming


THE SOLUTION (3 STEPS):
════════════════════════

STEP 1: Open Sound Settings
  Windows 10/11:
    1. Right-click speaker icon in taskbar
    2. Click "Open Sound settings"
    OR
    3. Settings → Sound → Advanced

STEP 2: Find Your Microphone
  1. Scroll down to "Advanced"
  2. Click "Volume mixer"
  3. Under "Advanced" section, find "App volume and device preferences"

STEP 3: Disable Monitoring
  Windows 10:
    1. Right-click your microphone device name
    2. Properties
    3. Microphone → Levels tab
    4. Look for "Microphone Boost" and LOWER it or disable
    5. Check "Advanced" tab for "Microphone monitoring" - set to OFF
  
  Windows 11:
    1. Settings → Sound → Advanced
    2. Scroll to "Microphone"
    3. Click your microphone
    4. Scroll down and find any "Listen to this device" option
    5. Turn it OFF
    OR
    1. Settings → Sound → Volume mixer
    2. Find your microphone
    3. Look for "Listen" or "Monitoring" options


DETAILED STEP-BY-STEP (Windows 11):
════════════════════════════════════

1. Click Start → Settings

2. Go to: System → Sound

3. Scroll down to "Microphone"

4. Click on your microphone device (e.g., "Microphone (Sound Blaster...)")

5. Click "Device properties"

6. In the window that opens:
   - Look for "Additional device options" or "Advanced"
   - Look for any of these options:
     * "Listen to this device"
     * "Microphone monitoring"
     * "Mic listen-back"
     * "Sidetone"
   - Set them ALL to OFF or DISABLED

7. Click "Apply" and "OK"

8. DONE!


DETAILED STEP-BY-STEP (Windows 10):
════════════════════════════════════

1. Right-click speaker icon in taskbar

2. Click "Open Sound settings"

3. Scroll down to "Related settings"

4. Click "Volume mixer"

5. Look for your microphone device in the list

6. Right-click it → Properties

7. Go to "Microphone" tab

8. Click "Levels" tab

9. Look for any settings like:
   - "Microphone Boost" - Set to MINIMUM or DISABLED
   - "Monitoring" or "Listen to this device" - Set to OFF

10. Click "Advanced" tab

11. Look for:
    - "Microphone monitoring" or "Listen to this device"
    - Set to OFF

12. Click "Apply" and "OK"

13. DONE!


ALTERNATIVE: DEVICE-LEVEL SETTINGS
═════════════════════════════════════

If Windows settings don't have the option:

Sound Blaster Audigy Control Panel:
  1. Right-click Sound Blaster icon in taskbar
  2. Open Sound Blaster Control Panel
  3. Look for "Microphone" or "Recording" settings
  4. Find "Monitor" or "Loopback" option
  5. Turn it OFF

Conexant Audio Settings:
  1. Control Panel → Sound
  2. Right-click Conexant microphone
  3. Properties
  4. Look for "Listen to this device"
  5. Uncheck it


IF OPTION NOT FOUND:
══════════════════════

Some audio devices don't have sidetone options. In that case:

Option 1: Update audio drivers
  1. Go to device manufacturer website
  2. Download latest drivers for your card
  3. Install and restart
  4. Check settings again

Option 2: Use audio device control panel
  Most Sound Blaster and Conexant devices have their own apps:
  1. Search for "Sound Blaster" or "Conexant" in Start menu
  2. Open their control panel
  3. Find microphone monitoring settings
  4. Disable it there

Option 3: BIOS/Hardware setting
  Some older systems have microphone monitoring in BIOS:
  1. Restart computer
  2. Press F2, DEL, or F10 (depends on motherboard) during startup
  3. Look for "Microphone Monitoring" or "Audio Settings"
  4. Disable it


TESTING IF IT WORKED:
══════════════════════

Before Fix:
  1. Speak into microphone
  2. You hear yourself through speakers
  3. Problem!

After Fix:
  1. Speak into microphone
  2. You DON'T hear yourself through speakers
  3. But game/Discord still receives your voice
  4. SUCCESS!


GAMING WORKFLOW (NOW):
═══════════════════════

1. Voice Isolator running (removes speaker game audio from your voice)
2. Microphone monitoring DISABLED (you don't hear yourself)
3. Game chat sends your voice to teammates ONLY
4. Teammates hear clean voice without game audio

Result: Perfect voice communication!


IF YOU WANT TO HEAR YOURSELF SOMETIMES:
════════════════════════════════════════

Some gamers like having sidetone at LOW volume to hear themselves.

To enable with LOW monitoring:

Windows:
  Microphone Properties → Levels tab
  → Look for "Microphone" slider
  → Keep it LOW (not maximum)

Sound Blaster/Device Control Panel:
  → Microphone Monitoring: ON
  → Monitor Level: LOW (usually 30-50%)


═════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SUMMARY OF WHAT TO DISABLE:
═════════════════════════════

Look for and DISABLE any of these settings:
  ✓ "Listen to this device"
  ✓ "Microphone monitoring"
  ✓ "Sidetone"
  ✓ "Mic listen-back"
  ✓ "Monitor this device"
  ✓ "Microphone loopback"

Usually found in:
  ✓ Windows Sound Settings
  ✓ Microphone Properties → Levels/Advanced
  ✓ Audio Device Control Panel (Sound Blaster, Conexant, etc.)
  ✓ Device manufacturer's software

1. Open Windows Sound Settings
2. Find your microphone
3. Open Microphone Properties/Device Properties
4. Find and DISABLE "Listen to this device" or "Monitoring"
5. Test: Speak into mic, you shouldn't hear yourself
6. Done!
